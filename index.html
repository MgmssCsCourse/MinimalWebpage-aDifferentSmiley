English words, that aren't used in everyday conversations

The word, its pronounciation, a description of it and the german translation
  • cantankerous:
    • an adjective, used to describe someone as being bad-tempered, argumentative and uncooperative.
    • The german translation is "streitsüchtig", which is probably used more often than the english word for it.
    • Cantankerous is pronounced: /kanˈtaŋk(ə)rəs/
  • fortnight:
    • a noun, with the meaning of a period of time of two weeks
    • It doesn't really have a german translation, but the closest one would be "vierzehn Tage" or "zwei Wochen".
    • fortnight is pronounced /ˈfɔːtnʌɪt/
  • petrichor:
    • a noun, standing for the smell that often occurs after the first rain, that comes after a long period of dry and warm weather.
    • In german, this is named "Petrichor" too, coming from the old greek.
    • It's pronounciation is /ˈpɛtrʌɪkɔː/
  • ethereal:
    • an adjective, describing something as delicate and light, seemingly not coming from this world.
    • The german word for it is "ätherisch".
    • It is pronounced /ɪˈθɪəriəl/
  • apricity:
    • a noun, standing for the sun in the winter
    • The german translation is "Aprizität"
    • It is pronounced /aˈprɪsɪti/
